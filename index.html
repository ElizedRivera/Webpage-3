Box 5

A long, legendary poem, written in 1938, about the illness of a group of miners in Gauley Bridge, West Virginia. Coming hot on the heels of modernist long poem masterpieces like Eliot’s The Wasteland or Stein’s Tender Buttons, the poem’s deliberate lucidity isn’t just an aesthetic choice—it’s a political one, Colleen Abel wrote in Ploughshares. Rukeyser, from the beginning of Book of the Dead, seeks the reader’s participation in the journey to Gauley Bridge. The reader is implicated from the first section, The Road,” in which Rukeyser calls outward to her audience: These are roads you take when you think of your country. The disaster Rukeyser is about to explore is a part of “our country and the reader will have no choice but to confront it. -Muriel Rukeyser
